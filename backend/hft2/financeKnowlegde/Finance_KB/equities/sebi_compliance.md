## SEBI LODR Regulations 2015 Overview

The Securities and Exchange Board of India (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015 governs all listed companies in India.

### Key Compliance Objectives
- Ensure investor protection
- Promote transparency in markets
- Standardize listing requirements across exchanges
- Strengthen corporate governance
- Enhance disclosure standards

---

## Initial Listing Requirements

### Eligibility Criteria for IPO

#### Financial Requirements
1. **Minimum Post-Issue Paid-up Capital:** ₹3 crore
2. **Minimum Public Offer Size:** ₹10 crore
3. **Minimum Public Shareholding:** 25% of post-issue capital
4. **Profitability Track Record:**
   - Profitable in at least 3 out of last 5 years
   - Minimum net worth of ₹1 crore in each profitable year

#### Asset Requirements
- **Net Tangible Assets:** At least ₹3 crore in each of preceding 3 years
- **Monetary Value Calculation:** Based on audited financial statements
- **Exclusions:** Revaluation reserves not included

#### Alternative Route (For Loss-Making Companies)
If company doesn't meet profitability criteria, must satisfy:
- Issue size ≥ ₹1000 crore (including fresh issue + offer for sale)
- Mandatory market-making for minimum 2 years
- Stricter disclosure norms

## Continuous Disclosure Obligations

### Annual Report Requirements

**Timeline:** Within 6 months from end of financial year

#### Mandatory Components

**1. Audited Financial Statements**
- Balance Sheet
- Statement of Profit & Loss
- Cash Flow Statement
- Notes to Accounts
- Auditor's Report with qualifications (if any)

**2. Board's Report**
- Directors' Responsibility Statement
- Management Discussion & Analysis
- Corporate Governance Report
- Related Party Transactions disclosure
- Particulars of employees (if applicable)
- Energy conservation, technology absorption details

**3. Corporate Governance Report**
- Board composition and committees
- Risk management framework
- Whistleblower mechanism
- Prevention of insider trading policy
- Material subsidiaries review

**4. Management Discussion & Analysis (MD&A)**
- Industry structure and developments
- Opportunities and threats
- Segment-wise performance
- Risk management policies
- Internal control systems adequacy
- Financial performance analysis
- Material developments in HR/IT

### Quarterly Results Requirements

**Timeline:** Within 45 days from quarter end

#### Required Disclosures
1. **Standalone and Consolidated Financial Results**
   - Both formats mandatory if company has subsidiaries
   
2. **Limited Review by Auditors**
   - Review report required
   - Modifications must be highlighted

3. **Statement of Assets and Liabilities**
   - Summarized balance sheet
   - Key line items

4. **Cash Flow Statement**
   - Operating, investing, financing activities
   - Direct or indirect method acceptable

5. **Significant Events During Quarter**
   - Material acquisitions/disposals
   - Litigation impacts
   - Changes in accounting policies

### Event-Based Disclosures (Immediate - Within 24 Hours)

Listed companies must immediately disclose material events:

#### Category A: Strategic Events
- Acquisition or disposal of undertaking exceeding 20% of assets
- Scheme of arrangement (merger, demerger, amalgamation)
- Revision in rating(s) assigned to instruments
- Fraud/defalcation by promoter/key managerial personnel

#### Category B: Operational Events
- Commencement of commercial production/power generation
- Capacity expansion/utilization
- New product launches with material impact
- Disruption of major operations
- Labor strikes/lock-outs affecting production

#### Category C: Financial Events
- Default in payment of interest/principal on loans/debentures
- Invocation of pledge by promoters exceeding threshold
- Giving guarantees/loans/investments beyond limits
- Write-off of loans/advances material in nature

#### Category D: Management Changes
- Resignation of statutory auditor/compliance officer
- Resignation of director(s)
- Change in key managerial personnel
- Appointment of CEO/CFO/Company Secretary

#### Category E: Legal/Regulatory
- Litigation/orders affecting business significantly
- Search/seizure operations by authorities
- Prosecution launched against company/directors
- Regulatory action by SEBI/RBI/other authorities

#### Category F: Market Related
- Change in shareholding pattern (promoter/promoter group)
- Buyback of securities
- Delisting proposals
- Voluntary winding up

## Corporate Governance Requirements

### Board Composition (Regulation 17)

#### Top 500 Listed Entities (By Market Cap)
- **Minimum Directors:** 6
- **Independent Directors:** At least 50% if chairperson is non-executive
- **Executive Chairperson:** At least 2/3rd independent directors
- **Woman Director:** At least 1 independent woman director

#### Other Listed Entities
- **Minimum Directors:** 3 (for all companies as per Companies Act)
- **Independent Directors:** 
  - Non-executive chairperson: At least 1/3rd independent
  - Executive chairperson: At least 1/2 independent

### Board Meetings

#### Frequency Requirements
- **Minimum Meetings:** 4 per year
- **Maximum Gap:** 120 days between two meetings
- **Notice Period:** 7 days (can be shorter with consent)

#### Meeting Conduct
- **Quorum:** 1/3rd of directors including at least 1 independent director
- **Participation:** Physical or through video conferencing
- **Minutes:** Recorded and maintained
- **Separate Meeting of Independent Directors:** At least once annually (without management presence)

### Board Committees (Mandatory)

#### 1. Audit Committee (Regulation 18)

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 members
- 2/3rd must be independent directors
- All members financially literate
- Chairman must be independent director
- Company Secretary acts as secretary

**Roles and Responsibilities:**
- Oversight of financial reporting process
- Appointment/remuneration of auditors
- Approval of related party transactions
- Scrutiny of inter-corporate loans/investments
- Evaluation of internal controls
- Review of quarterly/annual results
- Vigil mechanism establishment

**Meeting Requirements:**
- Minimum 4 meetings per year
- Maximum gap of 120 days
- CFO, auditors attend by invitation

#### 2. Nomination and Remuneration Committee (Regulation 19)

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 non-executive directors
- 2/3rd independent directors
- Chairman can be executive or non-executive

**Functions:**
- Formulate criteria for determining qualifications, positive attributes, independence
- Recommend appointment/removal of directors, KMP
- Formulate remuneration policy
- Evaluate board performance
- Succession planning

#### 3. Stakeholders Relationship Committee (Regulation 20)

**Composition:**
- Minimum 3 directors
- At least 1 independent director
- Chairman: Non-executive director

**Responsibilities:**
- Resolve grievances of security holders
- Monitor share transfer/transmission processes
- Review status of investor complaints
- Oversee share allotment/listing formalities

### Related Party Transactions (Regulation 23)

#### Definition of Related Parties (Ind AS 24)

**Persons Who Are Related Parties:**
1. Person with control/joint control over entity
2. Person with significant influence over entity
3. Member of key management personnel
4. Close family members of above persons
5. Entities where above persons have control/joint control/significant influence

**Types of RPTs Include:**
- Purchase/sale of goods or services
- Leasing arrangements
- Inter-corporate loans/guarantees
- Asset transfers
- Foreign currency translations
- Management contracts
- Research and development transfers

#### Materiality Thresholds (Amended 2022)

**Transaction-Level Materiality:**
A transaction is material if BOTH conditions met:
1. Transaction value exceeds 10% of annual consolidated turnover AND
2. Transaction value exceeds ₹100 crore

**Cumulative Materiality:**
All transactions with same related party during FY:
- Exceeds ₹1000 crore OR
- Exceeds 10% of annual consolidated turnover

#### Approval Process

**Stage 1: Audit Committee Approval (Mandatory for ALL RPTs)**
- Prior approval required before entering transaction
- Committee reviews necessity and commercial terms
- Ensures arm's length basis
- Can grant omnibus approval for repetitive transactions (valid for max 1 year)

**Stage 2: Shareholder Approval (For Material RPTs)**
- Required if transaction meets materiality thresholds
- Related parties cannot vote on resolution
- Approval through special resolution (postal ballot/e-voting)
- Filing with stock exchanges within 24 hours

#### Arm's Length Principle
- All RPTs must be at arm's length
- Terms comparable to unrelated third parties
- If not arm's length, detailed justification required
- Independent valuation may be needed

#### Disclosure Requirements

**In Annual Report (Form AOC-2):**
1. Details of all RPTs:
   - Name of related party and relationship
   - Nature of transactions
   - Transaction values
   - Amounts outstanding
   - Terms and conditions
2. Justification for non-arm's length transactions (if any)
3. Safeguards implemented

**Quarterly Compliance:**
- Summary of all RPTs entered during quarter
- Materiality assessment
- Approvals obtained
- Filing with stock exchanges

## Insider Trading Prohibitions (SEBI PIT Regulations 2015)

### Unpublished Price Sensitive Information (UPSI)

**Definition:**
Information relating to company/securities that:
- Is not generally available
- Upon becoming generally available, likely to materially affect price
- Relates to specific company/securities (not general market)

**Examples of UPSI:**
1. Financial results and dividends
2. Changes in key managerial personnel
3. Amalgamations, mergers, demergers, acquisitions
4. Significant changes in policies/plans/operations
5. Start of new projects
6. Material events per listing agreement
7. Fraud/default by company/promoters/directors
8. Any change in assets/liabilities exceeding 20%

### Who is an Insider?

#### Connected Person

**Direct Connection:**
1. Director/employee/officer of company
2. Person holding ≥10% shareholding (direct/indirect)
3. Person with contractual/fiduciary relationship
4. Government official/regulatory authority member
5. Family members/spouses of above persons

**Indirect Connection:**
6. Entities where connected persons have control/influence
7. Intermediaries (bankers, lawyers, consultants)
8. Printing press employees handling UPSI
9. Officials of stock exchanges/market regulators

#### Insider Definition
Any connected person who possesses UPSI

### Prohibited Activities

#### 1. Trading While in Possession of UPSI
- Cannot buy/sell/securities when possessing UPSI
- Applies even if UPSI not motivation for trade
- Strict liability - intent not required
- Includes pledging/invoking pledges

#### 2. Communication of UPSI (Tipping)
- Cannot communicate UPSI to others
- Exception: In furtherance of legitimate purposes
- **Legitimate Purposes Include:**
  - Discharging employment duties
  - Complying with legal obligations
  - Protecting own interests (legal/tax advice)
- Must maintain confidentiality

#### 3. Procuring Others to Trade
- Cannot induce others to trade based on UPSI
- Includes suggesting trades without disclosing UPSI
- Counseling/procurring prohibited
- Vicarious liability for entities

### Defenses Against Insider Trading

#### 1. Innocent Misappropriation Defense
Burden on accused to prove:
- Did not know information was UPSI
- Reasonable person wouldn't consider it UPSI
- Information already public or obtained legitimately

#### 2. Chinese Wall Defense
For organizations with multiple business verticals:
- Decision maker separate from UPSI holders
- Proper systems to prevent information flow
- Regularly reviewed and enforced procedures
- Training and compliance monitoring

#### 3. Trading Plans
- Pre-planned trades filed with compliance officer
- Minimum duration: 6 months
- Cannot modify/cancel plan once started
- Quarterly disclosures required
- Not available for designated persons with daily UPSI access

### Code of Conduct Requirements

#### Every Listed Company Must Have:

**1. Code of Internal Procedures**

**Key Elements:**
- Preservation of UPSI
- Communication protocols with analysts/media
- Disclosure to stock exchanges
- Maintenance of structured digital database
- Implementation of Chinese walls
- Employee training programs

**2. Designated Persons**

**Identification Criteria:**
- Based on function/role (access to UPSI)
- Includes directors, officers, employees
- Extended to immediate family members
- Applies to vendors/contractors with UPSI access

**Obligations:**
- Pre-clearance required for all trades
- Trading window restrictions
- Disclosure requirements
- Confidentiality maintenance

**3. Trading Window Restrictions**

**Closure Periods:**
- From end of every quarter till 48 hours after result declaration
- During material events (mergers, acquisitions, etc.)
- As decided by compliance officer

**Applies To:**
- All designated persons
- Their immediate family members
- Entities they control

**4. Pre-Clearance of Trades**

**Process:**
- Application in prescribed format
- Approval within 5 working days
- Validity: 7 days from approval
- Execution within validity period mandatory

**Conditions:**
- Minimum holding period: 6 months
- Cannot take opposite position within 6 months
- Contrarian trades restricted

### Disclosure Requirements

#### Initial Disclosure
- **Timeline:** Within 7 days of appointment/listing
- **Content:** Holdings of designated persons
- **Format:** PAN-wise disclosure
- **Threshold:** All holdings regardless of size

#### Continuous Disclosure
- **Timeline:** Within 2 working days of trade
- **Trigger:** Any trade by designated person
- **Details Required:**
  - Number of securities acquired/disposed
  - Nature of transaction (buy/sell/exercise of options)
  - Price
  - Post-transaction holding

#### Quarterly Disclosure
- **By:** Promoters/promoter group
- **Timeline:** Within 14 days of quarter end
- **Content:** Consolidated shareholding pattern
- **Includes:** Pledges created/received

### Surveillance and Enforcement

#### SEBI Investigation Powers
- Call for information/explanation
- Summon witnesses
- Search and seizure operations
- Impose interim orders (freeze assets/securities)
- Access electronic records

#### Penalties

**Civil Liability (Section 15G of SEBI Act):**
- Penalty up to ₹25 crore OR
- 3 times profit made from insider trading OR
- Whichever is higher

**Criminal Liability (Section 24 of SEBI Act):**
- Imprisonment up to 10 years OR
- Fine up to ₹25 crore OR
- Both

**Additional Consequences:**
- Debarment from securities market
- Disgorgement of ill-gotten gains
- Reputation damage
- Employment termination
